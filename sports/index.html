Sports

I ran track in middle school, but I never ran cross country. When I finished middle school I knew I was going to Riley High School due to its engineering magnet. Someone told me about the XC program there and how I should try it. I was skeptical at first, but I knew running XC would help me with track, so I decided to give it a shot. Boy am I glad I made that decision. Over time, I grew to love cross country more than I did track. I enjoyed the races, the team was amazing, and I had loads of fun each year. Overall I ran alright. I made varsity my junior year, and missed out senior year due to an injury. My all-time PR by the time I finished running cross ended up being an 18:42. I know that if I didn’t get injured I could’ve gone faster, but that is besides the point. To finish off my XC experience here at Riley, I was elected co-captain of the team my senior year by my fellow teammates. I really care about my teammates, and to have them continue on to do great things makes me happy. I wish them the best on the rest of their running careers.

I’ve been running track since 5th grade, and I have been pretty consistent with what events I have been running. I originally started off as a sprinter, because I thought I was fast (Which is also why I first joined the track team at Clay Middle). I quickly learned that I was not that fast and that I wasn’t going to do very well. I, for some reason, decided to try out shot put. I was actually decent at it, but during my 5th grade year, without me knowing, my coach put me in the 800. I was surprised and a little angry that I didn’t get a heads up, but after some time it became my best event. I found out that I wasn’t really meant for throwing or sprinting, but rather I was meant for middle and long distance running. I was the fastest 800 runner at my school and placed 6th overall in the city. In high school, I tried to continue running the 800, but Coach Wetzel made me mile and 2 mile instead. That was, until we ran an all out 300, and I placed 2nd to Jared (Our top runner), and I could’ve beat him too if I weren’t put in the back of the entire group. I guess Coach realized that maybe I do have some leg speed. Ever since, I have focused on the 800. I was never the best at it (My all-time PR is only a 2:12), but I always gave it my best. I made varsity my sophomore, junior, and senior years. I was an alternate my sophomore and junior years, but I actually raced my senior year and helped get my 4x800m relay to Regionals. A decent way to end my running career at Riley High School.
