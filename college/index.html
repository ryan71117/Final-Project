College

I’ve known for a long time now that I wanted to go to college. The only issue was that I didn’t know what I wanted to study, nor did I know where I wanted to go. I didn’t really have an idea until I entered high school. That was because I joined the engineering magnet at Riley High School, and I really enjoyed it. I decided I wanted to study a engineering. Step 1 was done. Now to figure out what type of engineering. They offer various types of engineering classes at Riley and I wanted to take a few for the general knowledge, and to narrow down the list of what I wanted to do. My favorite class was DE (Digital Electronics). I just really enjoyed everything we did in there from learning boolean algebra to wiring circuits. I decided to try to be an electrical/electronic engineer. I did some research on various colleges and ended up applying to 5 different universities. I applied to Purdue, Notre Dame, Rose-Hulman, Trine, and CU Boulder. I was accepted into CU Boulder (Exploratory Studies), Purdue (College of Liberal Arts), Trine (Electrical Engineering), and Rose-Hulman (Electrical Engineering). After a lot of evaluating, and college visits to a few of the colleges, I decided to go to Purdue. Why did I decide on Purdue when I wanted to go into college for electrical engineering, but was accepted into the College of Liberal Arts at Purdue? Well, when I visited Purdue I had an opportunity to meet with one of the counselors. We talked and at one point, he asked me about the classes I took, and which ones I enjoyed. I answered and and he eventually recommended majoring in Industrial Design. He explained what it was and I was very intrigued. After lots of thought on every aspect of the colleges (Location, Major, etc.), that is when I decided to attend Purdue. I am very excited to attend Purdue with several of my classmates, and I am also excited to see where the road takes me.
